# Reflection – CampusFix System

## Challenges Faced

One of the main challenges was balancing the needs of different stakeholders. Students required fast and simple issue reporting, while administrators required detailed reporting and system control. These needs sometimes conflicted, requiring careful prioritization.

Another challenge was designing a system that is scalable and maintainable while keeping the implementation feasible for an individual project. This required making architectural decisions such as modular design for future integrations (e.g., SMS and Email) without implementing them immediately.

## Trade-offs

A key trade-off was excluding direct SMS and Email integration due to cost constraints. Instead, a modular notification system was designed to support future integration while relying on internal messaging in the current system.

## Lessons Learned

This assignment highlighted the importance of clear and structured requirements. It demonstrated that a well-defined system must consider not only functionality but also performance, scalability, and maintainability.

It also reinforced the importance of aligning technical design decisions with stakeholder needs.
